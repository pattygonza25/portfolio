Client

Context

A Premium Product With No Digital Presence

Bdoors is a European interior door supplier operating in the South Florida B2B market — targeting architects, interior designers, contractors, real estate developers, and hospitality buyers across Miami-Dade and Broward County.

The product was genuinely premium: Spanish-engineered door systems, 15-year warranty, in-stock availability. The problem was that none of that came through digitally. The existing web presence had no growth infrastructure, no SEO architecture, no lead segmentation, and no CRM. Sales were managed manually with no pipeline visibility.

My mandate was to build the full digital growth system — and have it ready to support Google Ads campaigns before a single dollar of paid media was spent.

The Problem

Six Gaps That Were Costing Leads Every Day

The audit revealed a site that looked like a brochure and acted like one too. No mechanism to capture leads, no way to understand who was visiting, and no content that spoke to the professional buyer's actual language.

The biggest missed opportunity: five distinct professional segments — each with completely different motivations, timelines, and objections — were all being sent to the same generic contact form with zero segmentation.

  • No SEO architecture — vague URLs, missing meta tags, no keyword strategy
  • Generic content with no B2B value proposition for professional buyers
  • No differentiated product pages per collection
  • No blog or content to build organic authority
  • Single contact form with no lead segmentation — sales had zero visibility
  • No CRM — leads managed manually, no pipeline structure
The Solution

A Full Growth Stack, Built Before Ads Launch

01
Website Redesign & Visual System
Rebuilt on WordPress + Elementor with a consistent design system: Raleway 300/400, brand palette, and standardized structure across all 19 pages. Every section communicates premium positioning.
02
SEO Architecture
Restructured all URLs, configured title tags and meta descriptions via Rank Math on all pages, submitted XML sitemap, set up 301 redirects, and built an internal linking strategy across product and blog pages.
03
Product Pages & Industry Guides
One page per collection with targeted local keywords. Seven technical blog articles targeting long-tail B2B keywords — published before Google Ads launch to build topical authority first.
04
Segmented Lead Capture
Five dedicated landing pages — one per professional segment — each with a hidden field that auto-tags the lead on submission. Sales receives notifications with segment included. No manual sorting required.
05
HubSpot CRM Integration
Custom "Segment" property created in HubSpot Contacts. All form submissions sync automatically. Sales pipeline structured: New → Contacted → Proposal → Close. Full pipeline visibility from day one.
06
Security & Anti-Spam
Cloudflare Turnstile configured on all forms. Automatic confirmation emails to leads with catalogue CTA. Internal sales notifications with segment data. Every submission handled cleanly and professionally.

Lead Architecture

Five Segments. Five Landing Pages. Zero Manual Sorting.

The core insight driving the lead capture architecture: five professional segments visit the same site with completely different intentions. An architect specifying doors for a hotel renovation needs different information, different proof, and a different conversation than a homeowner finishing a Miami Beach penthouse.

Each landing page was built for Google Ads — Elementor Canvas template with no header or footer — optimized for conversion rather than navigation. A hidden field captures the segment automatically on form submission, routing the lead directly into HubSpot with full context for the sales team.

The general contact page uses a visible dropdown for organic visitors who self-identify. Two mechanisms, one clean pipeline.

Key Decisions

Why It Was Built This Way

🔍
Hidden field over dropdown on landing pages
Shorter forms convert better. The segment is captured automatically based on which landing page the lead arrived from. The dropdown is reserved for the general contact page where the traffic source is unknown — keeping each page optimized for its specific audience.
📝
7 blog articles before Google Ads launch
Building topical authority before paid traffic arrives means organic and paid reinforce each other. Google sees an established, credible site — not a brand new domain serving ads. The SEO groundwork makes every paid click more valuable.
🔗
CF7 over HubSpot native forms
HubSpot's free plan doesn't allow custom field mapping for non-HubSpot forms, and native HubSpot forms include branding that conflicts with premium B2B positioning. CF7 with the HubSpot plugin gives full design control without compromising the CRM integration.
📍
URLs without "miami" on landing pages
Avoided geo-locking the landing pages to a single city. This keeps them viable for broader South Florida targeting in Google Ads — Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Boca Raton, Palm Beach — without requiring separate pages per market.
