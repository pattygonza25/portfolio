Context

A Strong Product.
The Wrong Audience.

MiningStore is a U.S.-based Bitcoin mining company with $15M+ in revenue, 9+ years of operational history, and institutional-grade infrastructure across 11 sites in Iowa. Their offering — managed mining, hosting, and hardware — was genuinely differentiated.

The problem: their entire digital presence was built for crypto enthusiasts. The messaging spoke hashrate and block rewards. The content assumed technical knowledge. And the core retail audience was saturating.

Meanwhile, a new buyer was emerging: high-net-worth individuals, family offices, and private equity firms looking for asset-backed Bitcoin exposure with tax advantages. No one was speaking their language.

The Problem

Six Gaps in the Growth Engine

The audit revealed a site optimized for the wrong person. Technical jargon, retail-focused CTAs, and content that assumed crypto literacy. None of which resonated with a family office evaluating portfolio diversification or a tax consultant researching bonus depreciation.

There was no lead nurturing, no segmentation, and no system to move a prospect from first touch to booked call. Every lead went cold between discovery and sale.

  • Messaging built for crypto enthusiasts, not institutional investors
  • Core retail audience saturating, lower conversion potential
  • No lead nurturing, prospects went cold between touch and call
  • No ICP segmentation, one message for all audiences
  • LinkedIn inconsistent, no content calendar, no paid strategy
  • Zero GEO presence, no LLM-driven traffic at all
The Strategy

Reposition. Rebuild. Capture.

Three strategic priorities drove everything: reposition the brand for institutional audiences, rebuild the full-funnel infrastructure, and capture a new traffic channel before competitors discovered it.

01 / REPOSITION
Investor Language Over Crypto Language
Every piece of content was rewritten to speak ROI, yield, diversification, and tax efficiency. Not hashrate, block rewards, or mining difficulty. The audience was financial, not technical. We rebuilt the website, blog, and all messaging around five distinct investor segments.
02 / REBUILD
Full-Funnel Infrastructure From Zero
Complete SEO architecture rebuild. Three-layer lifecycle email system in Zoho Campaigns. Lead magnets per segment with dedicated landing pages. LinkedIn organic calendar plus geo-targeted paid campaigns. Automated lead scraping and enrichment for the sales team.
03 / CAPTURE
Pioneer GEO Before the Competition
Before GEO was an established term, structured all content to answer the questions HNWIs were asking AI tools. FAQs, detailed guides, investor-specific content optimized to be cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Zero-competition, high-intent channel.

Email Architecture

Three Layers. One System.
Right Message at Every Stage.

Rather than a single generic nurture flow, built three distinct automated systems — each serving a different funnel stage, each triggered by a different signal. The result: the right content reached the right prospect at the right moment, automatically.

Layer 01
Intent-Triggered Nurture
Trigger: Book a Call Click
Activated automatically when a prospect clicked "Book a Call" anywhere on the site. Educational sequence delivered before the sales call, reducing friction and improving call quality. Prospects arrived informed and pre-qualified.
Layer 02
Post-Call Segmented Nurture
Trigger: Sales Rep CRM Tag
Activated by the sales rep after tagging each lead by segment in Zoho CRM. Different sequences for family offices vs tax consultants vs real estate investors, each addressing their specific objections and motivations.
Layer 03
Weekly Broadcast
Trigger: Every Week
Weekly email to the full database combining awareness content — blog highlights, market insights, mining education — with a consistent CTA to book a call. Kept the brand top-of-mind across the full funnel, always-on.

GEO: Generative Engine Optimization

A New Channel Before
Anyone Had a Name for It

While reviewing analytics, a new traffic source appeared: ChatGPT. Small in volume, but the quality was unlike anything else. Institutional investors, family offices, tax consultants. The highest-intent visitors we'd seen.

The insight: LLMs cite content that answers specific questions, not content that ranks for keywords. So every blog post was restructured around the exact questions a HNWI investor would ask an AI tool. FAQs integrated into every page. Investor-specific guides built to be cited, not just read.

One lead told the sales team: "When I searched in Grok, MiningStore kept coming up. I checked the website and LinkedIn — the presence looked solid, so I booked a call."

That lead didn't come from an ad or SEO. It came from an AI referral — a channel with zero competition and maximum intent.

What drove the call growth
The 3.4x growth in qualified investor calls came from three converging factors: a clearer ICP that attracted the right visitors, a three-layer nurture system that pre-educated prospects before the call, and LinkedIn campaigns that put the sales team's face in front of the exact audience they needed to reach.
Scope of execution

Key Decisions

What Would I Do Differently

Improvement 01
Segment from the Landing Page, Not the Sales Call
The post-call nurture was triggered by the sales rep tagging the lead in Zoho after the call. More powerful: capture segment signals at the first landing page touch and start personalized nurture immediately, before any human interaction. This is now implemented for other clients using HubSpot.
Improvement 02
HubSpot Over Zoho for Attribution
Zoho's attribution reporting made it difficult to tie specific content pieces to booked calls. HubSpot's multi-touch attribution would have made optimization decisions faster and more precise — knowing exactly which blog post or email sequence drove which investor to book.
